OVERVIEW

Air pollution is one of the most pressing issues that the world is facing today. The vast amount of pollutants releasing into our atmosphere is damaging the air quality as well as the ozone layer. Many sources of the pollutants are man-made such as power plants, vehicle discharge, chemicals, waste, etc.

The detrimental impact of our environment has led to an aggressive progression in alternative energy and clean technology. Our proposal is to develop a building component system for high-rise buildings that cleans the environment and generates energy.

Buildings require a lot of energy for electricty, heating/cooling, and water/waste management. Large energy consumption leads to large emissions.

According to US Green Building Council, buildings (residential and commercial) accounted for 39% of total CO2 emissions (Industrial 29%, Transportation 33%)

To meet the population and economic growth, it is expected that at least 15 million new buildings will be constructed by the year 2015. Buildings can be a significant factor in reducing carbon emissions due its lifespan and scale/numbers.

Tech1: CO2 Capture System

Utilizing a developing carbon capture technology, we are now able to harvest one of the most abundant gases in our atmosphere.

The CO2 capture system engineered by Global Research Technologies captures CO2 at low stagnant air condition. Rather than targeting captures at low altitude, a strategy in taking advantage of altitude and scale of a high-rise building is considered. With heavy wind load on broad surfaces at higher altitudes of a high-rise building, carbon dioxide can be collected more effectively through natural direct intake.

The proposed system also recycles the carbon emission from the building itself to achieve zero carbon emission status.

Tech2: Algae Photobioreactor System

Over the years, there has been a significant development for algae bioreactors. There are variety of algae bioreactors ranging from the traditional open pond to closed photobioreactor systems.

The following factors are considered in determining the system that is best suited for the proposal:

  • low maintenance
  • controllable enclosure
  • requires less space

Photobioreactor system developed by Ohio University’s David Bayless proved to be the obvious choice. The system is simple, passing the collected CO2 through series of large fabric algae membranes. The membranes are easier to maintain, utilizing photosynthesis and water/nutrients to cultivate the algae.

Curtain Wall System Potential

With constant development of algae photobioreactors, the potential of integrating an energy producing/environment cleaning system is limitless.

Following are potential concepts that would make this algae photobioreactor facade even more effective:

  • Utilizing building’s own waste water/grey water as nutrient/water source.

  • Ability to convert algae oil to biodiesel on site to power the building itself. A building that’s able to produce and utilize its own resource cuts down carbon footprint associated with transporting resources.

  • By utilizing specific light-emitting diode (LED) that promotes greater algae growth, it creates unique design opportunity for building design.
