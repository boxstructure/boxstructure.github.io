SKY IS GREEN & TWINKIES ARE YELLOW

Before I get into my architectural education, my design foundation stemmed from my art education from my talented mother. My mother as a talented multidisciplinary artist encouraged me to open my mind and encouraged my use of imagination.

It all started when I started to color the sky green instead of blue in one of my drawings which provoked my mother to ask, “why is the sky green and not blue?”
I answered, “I don’t know.”
Rather than correcting my choice in color, she instead helped my thought process in why I might have picked green over blue. It turned out that I wasn’t drawing a scene of this world but an imaginary world in a different galaxy.

With such approach of not always having to conform to norms and exploring unconventional SOMETHING backed with rationale I’ve progressed and expanded my imagination I’ve progressed on with my art interests to various form of design.
